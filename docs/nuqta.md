Our Mission
Our mission is to provide consistent mentorship and
community for Muslim youth, rooted in Islamic principles, by
creating spaces that strengthen identity and connect youth
with role models, while developing their confidence and
leadership skills.
What is Nuqta.
Nuqta is a mentorship-based initiative focused on Muslim high school students, addressing the gap in support during a
critical stage of growth. It builds confident, grounded leaders through consistent mentorship, suhbah, and leadership
development.
3 pillars of Nuqta:
- Mentorship (Tarbiya)
- Community (Suhbah)
- Leadership Development 
Goals
1. Strengthen MSA leadership capacity
2. Promote spiritual growth during a formative stage
3. Foster meaningful peer companionship and suhbah
4. strengthen community connection through visibility, partnerships, and service
5. Ensure accessibility across diverse schools
6. Implement structured feedback and data driven insights for continuous
improvement

Why Nuqta?
Muslim youth are not lacking passion, they’re lacking structure and guidance.
Young leaders, mentors, and institutions already exist, but too often they operate in isolation. And
while there is no shortage of youth events, what’s missing are consistent spaces, real
mentorship, and strong suhbah.
Nuqta exists to bridge that gap.
We build a connected ecosystem, linking students to mentors, MSAs to resources, and
communities to each other, so that moments of engagement turn into sustained growth.
Through consistent mentorship, meaningful community, and leadership development, Nuqta
ensures young Muslims are not left to figure it out alone, but are developed into confident,
grounded leaders who will positively shape the Ummah.

What nuqta is and what it is not
What It Is:
● A starting point for identity, purpose, and leadership
● A community that develops leaders, not just
participants
● A space for consistent, youth-led events and
programming
● A community rooted in mentorship and meaningful
suhbah A bridge connecting students to mentors,
institutions, and role models
● A vehicle to improve your MSA,Youth group, or youth
organization
● A space for ALL Muslim youth—come as you are
What It Is NOT:
• A space defined by levels of religiosity or judgment
• A place for exclusivity, cliques, or social barriers
• A disconnected effort from masajid and institutions
A one-off program or event
2025–2026 Program Model
1. Trademark monthly (major) event
a. Our way of sharing and inviting the rest of the community and sharing this service with them
b. MSA-centered, community-informed and engaged
c. Allows leaders to test their learning from trainings (platforming them, acting upon what they learned)
2. Bi-weekly trainings and workshops for MSA leaders
a. Direct tarbiyyah and mentorship of MSA leaders
b. A space for board members; outcome is mentorship, training, advice, mock environment of leadership modules, helping navigate different aspects of leadership (spiritual, finances, marketing, internal disputes)
3. MSA visitations (speaking engagements, events)
a. SE, visiting high school meetings/gbms, 1-to-1 meetings with the board
b. This develops relationships, visibility, building bridges
4. Yearly Service Project
a. Khidmah beyond their halls
b. Fulfills their academic obligations
c. Investing back into the Muslim community, within local institutions, and to gain exposure to major non-profit relief groups, re-grounding of Muslim issues beyond their MSA
d. Creates opportunity for suhbah and prophetic style of convening community to serve humanity
e. Charity week/watermelon week for colleges to HS MSAs

Relationship with Masajid
● Masajid play a critical role in our communities, but they often operate with limited human capital and
multiple competing priorities.
● This makes it difficult to consistently focus on specialized youth leadership development at scale.
● Nuqta fills that gap—providing dedicated focus, structure, and continuity for youth growth.
● We don’t compete with masajid—we serve as a bridge, connecting youth to institutions and
amplifying both.
